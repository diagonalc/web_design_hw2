Stargazing

  • Place

    It is very important to choose a suitable place to have stargazing.
    This website provide a few nice spots,
    but if you can't go to these place, you still can enjoy it with a binocular in an open place that don't have serious light pollution.

  • What shoud you bring

    Many good spots for stargazing are remote, such as on the mountain or next to sea,
    so most importantly you should prepare food, water and clothes to keep warm.
    Also, you may need to bring power bank to prevent your phone goes out of battery.
    In places that is dark, you will need a flashlight. Remember, not the one on your phone.
    Use a red light when you reach the spot, it is the most basic and important rules.

  • Tools

    You may think that you must have a telescope to have stargazing. Actually, sometimes your eyes can be enough for you.
    If you want to have a better experience, a binocular that cost not much than $1000 can imporve a large step. Therefore, don't worry about the tools!
    tools that you really needs:
    • Star Map (can be either an app or a "real" map)
    • Compass (if you are using a "real" star map)
    • Flashlight (red or have a red glass paper)

  • Other

    • Beware of the weather!!! Always take a look on the weather.
      Hong Kong Observatory

    • Sometimes the place may be hot or wet, so you may need to prepare extra clothes for changes.

    • The website of Hong Kong Space Museum have some information about stargazing, such as the time of meteor shower and other astronomical event that Hong Kong can observe.
